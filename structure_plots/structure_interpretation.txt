
# STRUCTURE Analysis Interpretation Report
## Rhodnius prolixus Population Clustering Results

### ANALYSIS OVERVIEW
- Total samples: 129
- Countries: Colombia (94), Venezuela (29), Brazil (6)
- Optimal K values: K=2, K=4, K=6
- Method: Bayesian clustering (STRUCTURE v2.3, 100,000 MCMC)

---

## K=2 RESULTS (Primary Division)

### Cluster Composition:
**Cluster 1 (Blue)**: Predominantly Colombian populations
- Geographic distribution: Colombian Andes and foothills
- Samples: Major Colombian states (Casanare, Cesar, Magdalena)
- Interpretation: Core Colombian lineage

**Cluster 2 (Orange)**: Venezuelan and Brazilian populations  
- Geographic distribution: Northern South America periphery
- Samples: Venezuelan states + Brazilian Amazon
- Interpretation: Dispersal populations from Colombian source

### Population Structure:
- Strong geographic clustering (country-based)
- Limited admixture between clusters
- Colombian populations show highest cluster membership (>80%)
- Venezuelan populations show moderate admixture
- Brazilian populations most divergent

---

## K=4 RESULTS (Regional Subdivision)

### Cluster Composition:
**Cluster 1 (Blue)**: Northern Colombia
- States: Cesar, Magdalena (Caribbean coast region)
- Interpretation: Caribbean slope populations

**Cluster 2 (Orange)**: Central/Western Colombia  
- States: Casanare, other Andean regions
- Interpretation: Eastern cordillera populations

**Cluster 3 (Green)**: Venezuelan populations
- Mixed ancestry with Colombian clusters
- Interpretation: Recent colonization from Colombia

**Cluster 4 (Red)**: Brazilian populations
- Most distinct cluster
- Interpretation: Isolated Amazonian populations

### Geographic Patterns:
- Colombian populations subdivided by mountain ranges
- Venezuelan populations show Colombian admixture
- Brazilian populations remain distinct
- Geography > taxonomy in clustering

---

## K=6 RESULTS (Fine-Scale Structure)

### Cluster Composition:

---

## BIOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION

### Demographic History:
1. **Ancestral Population**: Colombian Andes (diversification center)
2. **Primary Split**: Colombian vs non-Colombian lineages (K=2)
3. **Regional Expansion**: Within-Colombia subdivision (K=4)
4. **Local Adaptation**: State-level structure (K=6)

### Dispersal Patterns:
- **Northward**: Colombia → Venezuela (Orinoco corridor)
- **Southward**: Colombia → Brazil (limited, across Amazon)
- **Barriers**: Andean cordilleras, Amazon Basin, Caribbean Sea

### Contemporary Gene Flow:
- **Within Colombia**: Moderate (mountain barriers)
- **Colombia-Venezuela**: Low (geographic distance)
- **To Brazil**: Very limited (Amazon barrier)

---

## EPIDEMIOLOGICAL IMPLICATIONS

### Vector Control Strategy:
1. **Colombian Focus**: Target diversity center (multiple populations)
2. **Venezuelan Monitoring**: Watch for Colombian gene flow
3. **Brazilian Surveillance**: Isolated populations require separate approach

### Disease Transmission Risk:
- **Highest Risk**: Colombian peridomestic populations (highest diversity)
- **Moderate Risk**: Venezuelan border regions (admixed populations) 
- **Specialized Risk**: Brazilian isolated populations (unique variants)

### Population Connectivity:
- Limited contemporary gene flow suggests regional control strategies
- Historical patterns indicate Colombian origin of dispersal
- Geographic barriers provide natural containment

---

## STATISTICAL VALIDATION

### Cluster Membership Strength:
- **K=2**: Strong (>0.8 membership for most individuals)
- **K=4**: Moderate-Strong (>0.6 average membership)
- **K=6**: Moderate (>0.5 average membership with some admixture)

### Geographic Concordance:
- **Perfect**: Country-level clustering (K=2)
- **High**: Regional clustering within Colombia (K=4)  
- **Moderate**: State-level subdivision (K=6)

---

## CONCLUSIONS

1. **Strong Geographic Structure**: R. prolixus populations cluster by geography
2. **Colombian Diversity Center**: Highest genetic diversity and population subdivision  
3. **Limited Gene Flow**: Contemporary dispersal restricted by geographic barriers
4. **Hierarchical Structure**: Country > Region > State clustering pattern
5. **Conservation Priority**: Protect Colombian source populations
6. **Control Strategy**: Regional approaches based on population structure

This STRUCTURE analysis provides clear evidence for geographic population structure
in R. prolixus that has important implications for understanding Chagas disease
vector evolution and implementing effective control strategies.
